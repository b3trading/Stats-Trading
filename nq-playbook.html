Phase 0
Pre-Market Context
Before 9:00am ET
Read the overnight before anything else. These observations set the probabilistic frame for the entire day. Do this before charts, before news, before anything.
P0
ALN Sessions · Overnight Read
Map the Asia & London session structure
Identify the Asia high and low. Did London stay inside Asia, break one side, or engulf both sides? This is your overnight narrative — write it down in one sentence.
🟢 London broke Asia High only NY tends to continue higher ~72% of the time. Lean long bias into the open. 72% Bull
🔴 London broke Asia Low only NY tends to continue lower ~68% of the time. Lean short bias into the open. 68% Bear
⚪ London inside Asia NY breaks London H or L 95% of the time. Compressed overnight = explosive open likely. Wait for direction at open. 95% Break
🟡 London engulfs Asia (both sides) Choppy overnight. No strong edge — NY continuation only ~55%. Reduce size, wait for IB clarity. 55% Cont.
P1
RTH Open Location · Prior Day Range
Mark yesterday's RTH high and low (9:30am–4:00pm)
Draw these two lines on your chart before the open. Where today opens relative to this range is your single most important structural fact of the morning.
⬆ Today opens ABOVE pRTH high (gap up) 83.29% chance price does NOT break back down through pRTH high. Strong bull continuation day — the gap tends to hold as support. 83% Bull
⬇ Today opens BELOW pRTH low (gap down) 83.29% chance price does NOT break back up through pRTH low. Strong bear continuation day — the gap tends to hold as resistance. 83% Bear
↕ Today opens INSIDE pRTH range 72.66% chance price breaks at least one side of pRTH before 4pm. No directional lean yet — wait for the IB to give direction. 73% Break
📝 Write your read State it plainly: "Open above pRTH = bull bias" or "Open inside = no bias yet, wait for IB." This one sentence is your anchor for the day.
Phase 1
Pre-IB Trade
9:30am → 10:30am ET
The window before Initial Balance is set. You have one setup here — the Morning Judas / 9:30 Continuation check. Execute this at 9:40am exactly. If conditions don't align, skip it entirely and move to Phase 2. Partial entries only — you're trading into an incomplete range.
01
9:30am · At the Bell
Note the exact 9:30 open price. Set a 10-minute timer.
Write down the 9:30 open. Do not trade. Let the first candle print. Your only job right now is to observe. The 9:30–9:40 window is the Judas candle — it will tell you continuation vs fake at 9:40.
02
9:40am · Judas Check
Where is price vs the 9:30 open? Apply the Judas probabilities.
At exactly 9:40, compare current price to your 9:30 open note. This is your first decision gate.
⬆ 9:40 price > 9:30 open (up move) Continuation more likely: 64% chance price stays above 9:30 open at 10:00am. Is this consistent with your ALN + pRTH bias? If YES → consider long. If NO → skip. 64% Cont.
⬇ 9:40 price < 9:30 open (down move) Continuation more likely: 62% chance price stays below 9:30 open at 10:00am. Is this consistent with your ALN + pRTH bias? If YES → consider short. If NO → skip. 62% Cont.
03
Confluence Gate · All 3 Must Align
Only take the pre-IB trade if ALL 3 signals point the same direction
This is your confirmation filter. You need: ALN session bias + pRTH open location + Judas direction — all three aligned. One misalignment = wait for Phase 2.
✅ All 3 Bullish → Long Setup London broke Asia high · Open above pRTH · 9:40 above 9:30 open. Proceed to the Long Trade Plan below. High Edge
✅ All 3 Bearish → Short Setup London broke Asia low · Open below pRTH · 9:40 below 9:30 open. Proceed to the Short Trade Plan below. High Edge
❌ Mixed signals → Skip Phase 1 entirely No trade. Close your order entry window. Mark your chart. Wait for 10:30am and move to Phase 2 where the IB gives you a cleaner, higher-probability setup. Skipping is a position. No Trade

Phase 2
Post-IB Trade
10:30am → Noon ET
The IB is now locked. This is where the statistics are clearest and the setups are highest probability. 96% of sessions will break the IB — your job is simply to trade in the direction it breaks, after it breaks. The edge is in how you confirm the break.
05
10:30am · Lock the IB
Record the exact IB High, IB Low, IB Range, and IB Midpoint
Draw all four levels on your chart. These are your anchors for the rest of the session. Calculate: IB Range = IB High − IB Low. IB Mid = (IB High + IB Low) ÷ 2. Then calculate your extension targets (+25%, +50%, +100% of range above IB High or below IB Low).
06
IB Close Position · Directional Bias
Where did price close when the IB window ended (10:30am candle close)?
The 10:30am candle's close position within the IB range is the most statistically reliable directional signal of the day. Upper half or lower half — this drives the 81%/74% bias.
⬆ 10:30 close in UPPER half of IB 81% probability the IB HIGH breaks before 4pm. Strong bull bias. Your trade is a long on the IB high break or a pullback toward IB mid. 81% High Breaks
⬇ 10:30 close in LOWER half of IB 74% probability the IB LOW breaks before 4pm. Strong bear bias. Your trade is a short on the IB low break or a bounce toward IB mid. 74% Low Breaks
07
Final Confluence Check · All Signals
Tally your 5 signals. Count bulls vs bears. Declare your session bias.
You now have 5 signals: ALN session, pRTH open, Judas direction, 9am hour close, IB close position. Count them. A 4-1 or 5-0 alignment is a high-conviction trade. A 3-2 split is acceptable. 2-3 or worse = reduce size significantly or skip.

08
IB Break Entry · The Trigger
Wait for price to actually BREAK the IB high or low. Then enter.
Do not anticipate the break — wait for a 1-minute or 5-minute candle to close outside the IB. A close outside is more reliable than a wick. The break should happen in the direction of your bias (Step 06 + 07). A break against your bias is a warning — skip or reverse only with extreme caution.
⬆ IB High breaks → Long Entry Enter long on the break (or retest of IB high from above). Stop below IB high. 82% chance the IB Low is NOT also broken — the single break tends to hold direction. 82% Single Break
⬇ IB Low breaks → Short Entry Enter short on the break (or retest of IB low from below). Stop above IB low. 82% chance the IB High is NOT also broken — trend your position. 82% Single Break
09
Post-IB Targets · Extension Levels
Set your targets using IB extension mathematics before you enter
Know your exits before your entry. Calculate all extension levels from the IB range. Scale out — don't hold for a single target. The 50% extension is your bread and butter; anything beyond is a bonus.
📐 Long Extension Targets (from IB High) T1: IB High + 25% of range (~50% by noon) · T2: IB High + 50% of range (~45% by close) · T3: IB High + 100% of range (~20% by close). Scale: 50% at T1, 30% at T2, 10% at T3. Scale Out
📐 Short Extension Targets (from IB Low) T1: IB Low − 25% of range (~50% by noon) · T2: IB Low − 50% of range (~45% by close) · T3: IB Low − 100% of range (~20% by close). Scale: 50% at T1, 30% at T2, 10% at T3. Scale Out

10
Noon Curve Awareness · 12pm–2pm
If still in a trade past noon — watch for the midday reversal zone
If you have a runner from either phase, be aware that daily highs and lows commonly form in the Q2/Q3 window (10am–2pm). If price stalls or wicks sharply near noon and you're at a target zone — consider taking profits. The Q4 (2pm–4pm) often reverses the Q3 extreme.
🕛 12pm–2pm: Hold or Protect? If you're at T2 or beyond and price is stalling near noon — move stop to T1. Protect the gain. If price is still accelerating through noon — hold the runner with a trailing stop.
🕑 2pm–4pm: Power Hour If you're flat at 2pm, the Power Hour can offer a second entry. Check: is the 9am hour signal (bull/bear 70%) still playing out? Trade in the direction of the confirmed session bias.

THE NON-NEGOTIABLES

1
Phase 1 requires all 3 signals aligned. Two out of three is not a trade. One misalignment and you wait for Phase 2. The IB setup is cleaner — save your ammunition.
2
Phase 1 size is always half. IB is not set. You are trading into an incomplete range with higher uncertainty. Half size, every time, no exceptions.
3
If the Judas reverses — exit immediately. The 9:30 open is your anchor. If price crosses back through it against your position, the Judas has activated. Out. No averaging.
4
96% break rate does NOT mean the break goes your direction. Wait for the IB close position bias (Steps 06) before entering. The 96% tells you it will break — the IB close position tells you which side.
5
If price returns inside the IB after breaking — flat everything. A failed IB break (price breaks then returns inside) is a warning of a potential double-break day (23% of sessions). The edge is gone — exit.
6
A 2-3 signal split means quarter size or no trade. When your 5 signals are mixed, the statistical edge is minimal. Reduce to quarter size or pass entirely. There will be a better day.
7
Know your IB extension targets BEFORE you enter. If you haven't calculated T1, T2, T3 before hitting the buy or sell button, you are not ready to trade.
