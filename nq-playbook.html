Pre-Market Context
Before 9:00am ET
Read the overnight before anything else. These two reads set the probabilistic frame for the entire day — before news, before charts, before anything.
01
ALN Sessions · Overnight Structure
How did London trade relative to Asia?
Identify the Asia high and low. Where London closed relative to the Asian range is your overnight narrative — one of four structures.
🟢 London broke Asia High only NY tends to continue higher ~72% of the time. Lean long into the open. 72% Bull
🔴 London broke Asia Low only NY tends to continue lower ~68% of the time. Lean short into the open. 68% Bear
⚪ London inside Asia NY breaks the London H or L 95% of the time. Compressed overnight = explosive open likely. Wait for direction. 95% Break
🟡 London engulfs Asia (both sides) No strong edge — NY continuation only ~55%. Reduce size, wait for IB clarity. 55% Cont.
02
RTH Open Location · Prior Day Range
Where does today open relative to yesterday's RTH high and low?
Mark yesterday's 9:30am–4:00pm high and low on your chart. Where today opens against those two lines is the most important structural fact of the morning.
⬆ Opens ABOVE pRTH high (gap up) 83.29% chance price does NOT break back through pRTH high. Gap tends to hold as support. Strong bull continuation bias. 83% Bull
⬇ Opens BELOW pRTH low (gap down) 83.29% chance price does NOT break back through pRTH low. Gap tends to hold as resistance. Strong bear continuation bias. 83% Bear
↕ Opens INSIDE pRTH range 72.66% chance price breaks at least one side of pRTH before 4pm. No directional lean yet — wait for IB direction. 73% Break
Pre-IB Trade
9:30am → 10:30am ET
One setup window — the First 10-Minute Candle check at 9:40am. The three pre-IB signals that must all align are: (1) ALN bias · (2) pRTH open location · (3) first 10-min candle direction. Any one misalignment = skip to Post-IB. Half-size only — IB range is still forming.
03
9:30am · At the Bell
Note the exact 9:30 open price. Do not trade. Set a 10-minute timer.
Write down or mark the 9:30 open on your chart. This price is your anchor for the first 10-minute candle check at 9:40am. Do not enter any position yet.
04
9:40am · First 10-Min Candle Check
At exactly 9:40am — where is price vs the 9:30 open?
Compare current price to your 9:30 anchor. Does the initial 10-minute move align with your ALN and pRTH bias from Steps 01–02?
⬆ 9:40 above 9:30 open (up move) Continuation more likely: 64% chance price stays above 9:30 at 10:00am. Does this match your ALN + pRTH bias? If YES → consider long. If NO → skip. 64% Cont.
⬇ 9:40 below 9:30 open (down move) Continuation more likely: 62% chance price stays below 9:30 at 10:00am. Does this match your ALN + pRTH bias? If YES → consider short. If NO → skip. 62% Cont.

All three must point the same direction to take the pre-IB trade. One misalignment = no trade, move directly to Step 07 (Post-IB).

06
10:00am · 9am Hour Close
Did the 9am candle (9:00–10:00am) close green or red?
Strongest single directional signal of the morning. Green close → NY session closes green 70% of the time. Red close → 70% bear. This confirms or contradicts your pre-IB trade.
Post-IB Trade
10:30am → Noon ET
IB is now locked. 96% of sessions break the IB — your job is to determine which side, confirm with your full signal tally, and trade the break with pre-calculated extension targets.
07
10:30am · Lock the IB
Record IB High, IB Low, IB Range, and IB Midpoint. Draw all four on your chart.
Range = IB High − IB Low. Mid = (IB High + IB Low) ÷ 2. Pre-calculate extension targets (+25%, +50%, +100% from the IB High or Low) before answering Step 08.
08
IB Close Position · Directional Bias
Where did price close when the IB window ended at 10:30am?
The closing position within the IB range is the most statistically reliable directional signal of the session — upper half or lower half drives the 81% / 74% break bias.
⬆ Closed UPPER half of IB range 81% probability the IB high breaks before 4pm. Strong bull bias — trade long on the break or on a pullback toward IB mid. 81% High Breaks
⬇ Closed LOWER half of IB range 74% probability the IB low breaks before 4pm. Strong bear bias — trade short on the break or on a bounce toward IB mid. 74% Low Breaks

IB Break Entry · The Trigger
Did price break the IB high or low? Which side, and was it in your bias direction?
Wait for a 1-min or 5-min candle to CLOSE outside the IB — a close is more reliable than a wick. The break should match your bias from Steps 08 and 09.
11
Post-IB Targets · Extension Levels
Set your targets using IB extension math before you enter. Know your exits before your entry.
Scale out — never hold for a single target. The +25% extension is your bread-and-butter T1. Anything beyond +50% is a bonus runner.
📐 Long Extension Targets (from IB High) T1: IB High + (Range × 0.25) · T2: IB High + (Range × 0.50) · T3: IB High + (Range × 1.00). Scale 50% at T1, 30% at T2, trail 10% at T3. Scale Out
📐 Short Extension Targets (from IB Low) T1: IB Low − (Range × 0.25) · T2: IB Low − (Range × 0.50) · T3: IB Low − (Range × 1.00). Scale 50% at T1, 30% at T2, trail 10% at T3. Scale Out

12
Noon Curve · 12pm–2pm Awareness
If still in a trade past noon — watch for the midday reversal zone.
Daily highs and lows commonly form in the Q2/Q3 window (10am–2pm). If price stalls or wicks sharply near noon at a target zone — consider protecting profits. The Q4 (2pm–4pm) often reverses the Q3 extreme.

THE NON-NEGOTIABLES

1
Pre-IB trade requires all 3 signals aligned. ALN bias + pRTH open + first 10-min candle direction. Two out of three is not a trade. One misalignment — skip to Post-IB.
2
Pre-IB size is always half. IB is not set. You are trading into an incomplete range. Half size, every time, no exceptions.
3
If price crosses back through the 9:30 open — exit immediately. The first candle fake-out has activated. Do not average. Do not wait. Out.
4
96% break rate does not mean the break goes your direction. The IB close position (Step 08) tells you which side. Wait for it before entering any post-IB trade.
5
If price returns inside the IB after breaking — flat everything. A failed IB break signals a potential double-break day (23% of sessions). The edge is gone.
6
2 or fewer aligned signals = no trade. Skipping is a position. There will be a better setup tomorrow. Preserving capital is the trade.
7
Know T1, T2, T3 before you enter. If extension targets are not calculated before you touch the order entry, you are not ready to trade.
