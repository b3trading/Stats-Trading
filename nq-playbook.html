Pre-Market Context
Before 9:00am ET
Read the overnight before anything else. These two reads set the probabilistic frame for the entire day — before news, before level-setting, before anything else.
01
ALN Sessions · Overnight Structure
How did London trade relative to Asia?
Identify the Asia high and low. Classify what London did — this is your overnight narrative, one of four structures.
02
RTH Open Location · Prior Day Range
Where does today open relative to yesterday's RTH high and low?
Mark yesterday's 9:30am–4pm high and low on your chart before the open. Where today's session opens against those two lines is the single most important structural fact of the morning.
Pre-IB Trade
9:30am → 10:30am ET
One setup window — the Morning Judas check at 9:40am. All three signals (ALN + pRTH + Judas direction) must align. If they don't, skip to Post-IB. Trade half-size only — the IB range is still forming.
03
9:30am · At the Bell
Note the exact 9:30 open price. Do not trade. Set a 10-minute timer.
Write down or mark the 9:30 open candle. This price is your Judas anchor — the entire pre-IB decision at 9:40 is measured against it.
04
9:40am · Judas Check
At exactly 9:40am — where is price vs the 9:30 open?
Compare current price to your 9:30 anchor. Does the initial move align with your ALN and pRTH bias from Steps 1–2?
05
Pre-IB · Trade Decision
Do all 3 signals align? ALN + pRTH open + Judas direction.
Confluence gate — all three must point the same direction. One misalignment means no pre-IB trade. Move to Step 7.

06
10:00am · 9am Hour Close
Did the 9am candle (9:00–10:00am) close green or red?
Strongest single directional signal of the morning. Green = NY closes green 70% of the time. Red = bear 70%. Either confirms your pre-IB trade or warns you to manage it.
Post-IB Trade
10:30am → Noon ET
IB is now locked. This is where the statistics are clearest. 96% of sessions break the IB — your job is to determine which side, confirm with your signal tally, then trade the break with pre-calculated targets.
07
10:30am · Lock the IB
What is the IB High, IB Low, IB Range, and IB Midpoint?
Draw IB High, IB Low, and IB Mid on your chart. Calculate: Range = High − Low. Mid = (High + Low) ÷ 2. Pre-calculate extension targets (+25%, +50%, +100%) before proceeding to Step 8.
08
IB Close Position · Directional Bias
Where did price close when the IB window ended at 10:30am?
The 10:30am candle's closing position within the IB range is the most statistically reliable directional signal of the session — upper half or lower half drives the 81% / 74% break bias.

IB Break Entry · The Trigger
Did price break the IB high or low? Which side, and was it in your bias direction?
Wait for a 1-min or 5-min candle to CLOSE outside the IB — a close is more reliable than a wick. The break should match your bias from Steps 8 and 9.

THE NON-NEGOTIABLES

1
Pre-IB trade requires all 3 signals aligned. Two out of three is not a trade. One misalignment — skip to the IB section. The IB setup is cleaner. Save your ammunition.
2
Pre-IB size is always half. The IB is not set. You are trading into an incomplete range. Half size, every time, no exceptions.
3
If price crosses back through the 9:30 open — exit immediately. The Judas has activated. Do not average. Do not wait. Out.
4
96% break rate does not mean the break goes your direction. The IB close position tells you which side. Wait for Step 8 before entering any post-IB trade.
5
If price returns inside the IB after breaking — flat everything. A failed IB break signals a potential double-break day (23% of sessions). The edge is gone.
6
2 or fewer aligned signals = no trade. Skipping is a position. There will be a better setup tomorrow.
7
Know T1, T2, T3 before you enter. If extension targets aren't calculated before you touch the order entry, you are not ready to trade.
