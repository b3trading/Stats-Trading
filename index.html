NOON CURVE
The midday reversal phenomenon quantified. The Noon Curve window is 8am–4pm ET, providing a framework for when the daily high/low forms, how far from the 8am open, and quarterly confluence analysis.
Midday Structure · 8am–4pm

QUARTERLY TIME WINDOWS (8am–4pm · 4 Quarters of 2 Hours)

Q1 — 8:00am to 10:00am
8AM–10AM
Pre-market + IB formation period. High-volatility, directional bias established. High-probability time for early highs/lows on trending days.
Q2 — 10:00am to 12:00pm
10AM–12PM
Post-IB continuation or reversal zone. Many daily extremes form in this window on normal sessions. AM session close.
Q3 — 12:00pm to 2:00pm
12PM–2PM
The Noon Curve reversal window. Low-volume, liquidity hunt. Common area for daily high or low to form before PM reversal.
Q4 — 2:00pm to 4:00pm
2PM–4PM
Power hour / PM session. Directional move into close. Often completes the Noon Curve reversal by closing opposite the noon extreme.

MetricDataTrading Implication

NOON CURVE CONCEPT: The 8am open acts as an anchor. Highs and lows that form between 8am–4pm can be statistically categorized by which quarter they appear in. The "Noon Curve" describes the tendency for price to make a high or low near noon (Q2/Q3), then reverse into the close (Q4). Combining this with quarterly confluence — i.e., which quarter the extreme is expected in based on session context — improves trade framing and price expectations for positions carried through lunch.

HOUR STATS
Every hour of the NQ trading day dissected. If the current hour opens inside the prior hour, and then sweeps the prior hour's high or low — what is the probability of price returning to the current hour's open? Segmented by 20-minute periods within each hour.
Sweep & Retrace · By Hour

SWEEP RETRACEMENT CONCEPT

The Framework If the 10am hour opens inside the 9am hour, and then price takes the 9am high (sweeps it) — there is historically a HIGH probability that price returns to the 10am open.

Time of sweep matters: Sweeping at 10:05 vs 10:45 produces different retrace probabilities. The earlier in the hour the sweep occurs, the higher the probability of a retrace back to the hour's open.

RULE OF THUMB: The earlier in the hour the sweep occurs, the more time remains for a retrace back to the hour open. A sweep at :05 into the hour with 55 minutes remaining has statistically better retrace probability than a sweep at :50 with only 10 minutes remaining. Use this to scale position size and target expectations on hour-open sweep setups.
